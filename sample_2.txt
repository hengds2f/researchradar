ABSTRACT
In this study, we propose a new meta-learning approach for few-shot image classification. Our method scales seamlessly.

INTRODUCTION
Prior models fail when restricted to very low numbers of examples.

METHODS
We designed a convolutional neural network (CNN) integrated with a memory-augmented module. The experiment utilized PyTorch over 4 A100 GPUs.

RESULTS
Our approach outperforms the state-of-the-art by 4.2% on the benchmark datasets. Both recall and precision exhibit substantial gains.

DISCUSSION
This provides strong evidence that memory augmentation benefits few-shot tasks. A limitation is the increased inference latency, which requires further optimization before real-world deployment.
