Section Classifier

SciBERT-style zero-shot classification assigns scientific section labels with confidence scores.

How this works

This tool reads every paragraph in a paper and uses machine learning to classify it:

  • Each paragraph is labelled with a section type (e.g., Introduction, Methods, Results)
  • A confidence score (0–100%) shows how certain the model is about each label
  • Two labels are shown: the original heading found in the paper, and the AI's predicted label
  • This helps you quickly understand the structure of an unfamiliar paper

    Agent Workflow

    Run a multi-agent research pipeline: Router → Retrieval → Synthesis → Critic → Writer.

    How the 5-agent pipeline works

    Five specialised AI agents work together in sequence to produce a high-quality, well-evidenced answer:

    🧭 Router — Reads your question and decides the best strategy (e.g. compare methods, synthesise findings, or answer directly)
    🔍 Retrieval — Searches all your uploaded papers to find the most relevant paragraphs and quotes
    🧠 Synthesis — Reads the retrieved passages and drafts a comprehensive, cited answer
    🔬 Critic — Reviews the draft for weak claims, unsupported statements, or logical gaps, and scores its quality
    ✍️ Writer — Rewrites the final response incorporating the critic's feedback for clarity and accuracy
    🧭 Router pending Decides the best strategy to answer your question
    🔍 Retrieval pending Searches your papers for the most relevant evidence
    🧠 Synthesis pending Combines the evidence into a coherent drafted answer
    🔬 Critic pending Reviews the draft for weak claims and missing evidence
    ✍️ Writer pending Polishes the final response for clarity and accuracy

    Provenance Ledger

    Tamper-evident hash chain for uploaded papers and generated outputs.

    How this works

    Every action in ResearchRadar is automatically recorded with a unique digital fingerprint:

    • When you upload a paper, a fingerprint (hash) of its content is saved
    • When the AI generates a summary or answer, that output is also fingerprinted and linked to the previous record
    • Each new record includes the previous record's hash — creating a chain that cannot be silently altered
    • If any record is tampered with after the fact, the chain verification will flag it as compromised
    • Think of it like a bank statement for your research activity — a permanent, tamper-proof audit trail

    Reading the timeline

    Each card in the timeline represents one recorded event:

    • 📄 Upload — Records when a paper was added and its content fingerprint
    • 📝 Summary — Records when the AI generated a synthesis or analysis output
    • 🤖 Agent Output — Records the result of an agent workflow run, including its quality score
    • The Content Hash is a fingerprint of the actual data; the Record Hash links this record to the previous one in the chain
    • When a real blockchain is configured, Tx Hash becomes a live link to the block explorer
    • When IPFS is enabled, IPFS CID becomes a link to the public gateway so anyone can retrieve the file
    • Click the copy button (⧉) next to any hash to copy it to your clipboard
